Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the questions I hear most often.

1. Do you guarantee we will get the grant?

+

No ethical grant writer can or should guarantee an award. Funding decisions are subjective and often based on factors outside our control (e.g., a funder's internal portfolio balance). What I do guarantee: Your proposal will be professionally written, 100% compliant with guidelines, and submitted on time. My clients win because we stack the deck with quality.

2. How long does the process take?

+

For a standard foundation proposal, plan for a minimum of 4-6 weeks from first strategy call to submission deadline. Rushed jobs (under 2 weeks) incur a rush fee and depend on your team's ability to provide data immediately. Federal grants require 8-12 weeks minimum.

3. What do you need from me to get started?

+

I need access to your financials (Audit or 990), your Board of Directors list with affiliations, and a 45-minute call with your Program Director. If you cannot provide these three things, we cannot start writing effectively.

4. This seems expensive. Why shouldn't I just hire an intern?

+

An intern costs you time (training, editing, re-writing). A professional costs money but saves you time and earns you more money. If a $5,000 grant writing fee helps you secure a $100,000 grant, the ROI is 20x. An intern will likely help you secure $0 because they lack the strategic writing skills to score high on a rubric.

5. Do you only write grants for U.S. organizations?

+

I primarily work with U.S.-based 501c3 nonprofits and U.S.-registered small businesses. However, I work extensively with Fiscally Sponsored projects and international NGOs applying for U.S. Government funding (USAID, State Dept).

6. Who owns the grant content after it's written?

+

You do. 100%. The narrative, the language, the data presentation—it's all yours. I encourage you to reuse the boilerplate language I craft for future applications. I'm building your capacity, not holding you hostage.

7. I have an emergency deadline. It's due in 5 days. Can you help?

+

Possibly. This depends entirely on my current workload and the complexity of the application. A 5-day turnaround requires a premium rush fee (+50% of project total). Please email me immediately with "URGENT DEADLINE" in the subject line.

8. What happens if we get funded?

+

We celebrate! Then we talk about the Stewardship Report. This is the most overlooked part of the process. I can assist with drafting the required interim and final reports to ensure you remain in good standing for future funding cycles.

9. Do you work on commission (percentage of the grant)?

+

No. I operate strictly on a Fee-for-Service basis. Grant Professionals Association (GPA) ethics strictly prohibit commission-based pay as it creates a conflict of interest and can jeopardize a nonprofit's tax-exempt status with the IRS.

10. What types of grants do you NOT write?

+

I do not write grants for political campaigns, religious proselytizing activities (faith-based social services are fine), or individual scholarships (except institutional scholarships).
